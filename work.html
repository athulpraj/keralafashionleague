KFL Rice Bucket Challenge.



The Rice Bucket Challenge initiative was pioneered by a Hyderabad based Journalist named Manjulatha Kalanidhi. She considered this initiative an inexpensive and productive alternative to the Ice Bucket Challenge, which involved participants pouring ice water over themselves, so as to create awareness about a disease called ALS. The Rice Bucket challenge inspires individuals to donate rice to a deprived person or organisation and subsequently invite more donors, to form a chain. Abhil Dev was moved by this initiative and thus, decided to propagate it across Kerala.

With the help of his experience and connection in both the film and fashion industries, as well as several social welfare organisations, he conceived the ‘KFL Rice Bucket Challenge’ in over 60 educational institutions, shopping malls and other forums across the State. The campaign gained mass support among the youth and celebrities, and has to date, donated over 2 lakh kilos of rice to orphanages, old age homes and also to people and places requested by the donors.

Manju Latha Kalanidhi is a journalist based in Hyderabad. Born and brought up in Telangana, this 38-year-old journalist has worked with mainstream English media in Hyderabad for the last 17 years.


On August 22, 2014, she started a social initiative called Rice Bucket Challenge which inspires people across the world to donate rice to the needy and help curb hunger. She has been reading about the ALS Ice Bucket Challenge on Facebook. The ALS Ice bucket challenge is a worthy challenge, no doubt but it was not really taken up by many Indians seriously. It is definitely a good cause in the US and is relevant to them, but when several people in Manju’s friends circle started taking it without any commitment, but just frivolously for timepass, that got her thinking. She thought that aping everything western, especially what is not relevant, Iis not good. As she was working for a rice website and was frequently writing article son rice, rice production, rice scarcity in several countries, especially in Asia and Africa, she started thinking abt it and decided to covert ICE Bucket Challenge into Rice Bucket Challenge. She also did not like wasting water and decided that she will start the challenge called Rice Bucket challenge where people will donate one bucket of rice to a needy person, click the photo and post it on the facebook nominating three more to do this chain donation.


Kerala has been in the forefront in joining the rice bucket challenge. Mr Abhil Dev from Kerala and his project Kerala Fashion League have taken the challenge committedly and has ensure that donations of more than 2,00,000 kg has happened so far in Kerala. Abhil has been travelling to various towns and cities, speaking to schools and colleges to motivate them to join the challenge. He has also promoted the cause extensively during the Kerala Fashion league shows, thereby effectively blending a good cause with his own brand. He has amply shown that it is possible to take forward a cause to a great level. He intends to take the Rice Bucket Challenge to more cities and has planned a mega challenge at Palakkad in January 2016.

So far 66,000 people across the globe have joined her in this social mission and close to 2,00,000 kgs of rice has reached the poor. The Rice Bucket Challenge celebrated 100 days of existence on November 30, 2014 and already an organization from Kolkata has pledged 50,000 kg for the milestone. The social service project has not spent any money on advertising or promotion and it is being considered as one of the biggest online movements in the global history.


Manju Latha Kalanidhi has been awarded the Karamveer Chakra award instituted by the United Nations and icongo (intl confederation of NGOs). She has received this award on March 23, 2015. She has also received the ‘Women Achiever award for social service’ by JFW magazine in Chennai.
